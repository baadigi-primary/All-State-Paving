# How Much Does Parking Lot Paving Cost in Central Ohio?

## Quick Answers

**How much does it cost to pave a parking lot in Central Ohio?**
A new asphalt parking lot in Central Ohio typically costs **$3 to $7 per square foot**, so a 10,000-square-foot lot generally runs **$30,000 to $70,000**. Resurfacing an existing lot with a solid base costs less — usually **$2 to $4 per square foot**. The final number depends on the condition of the base, the asphalt thickness your traffic requires, drainage work, and striping.

**Is it cheaper to resurface or replace a parking lot?**
Resurfacing is significantly cheaper than full replacement — often 40 to 60 percent less — but it only works when the underlying base is still sound. If the base has failed (you'll see alligator cracking, potholes, and areas that sink underfoot), an overlay will fail with it. A reputable contractor will core or probe the base before recommending either option.

**How long does a commercial parking lot last?**
A properly built asphalt parking lot lasts **20 to 30 years** in Ohio when it's sealcoated every 2 to 3 years and cracks are sealed as they appear. Skipping maintenance can cut that lifespan in half because of the state's freeze-thaw cycles.

## What Actually Drives Parking Lot Paving Cost

After more than 40 years paving lots across [Central Ohio](/areas-served), we can tell you the per-square-foot number you see online only matters once you understand what moves it. Two lots of the same size can differ by tens of thousands of dollars. Here's why.

### 1. New construction vs. resurfacing vs. repair
This is the biggest cost lever, and it comes down to the condition of your existing surface:

- **New construction** (full excavation, graded stone base, new asphalt) — the highest cost, but the only right answer when there's no usable base.
- **Resurfacing / overlay** (1.5"–2" of new asphalt over a sound base) — a like-new surface for a fraction of replacement cost.
- **Patching and crack sealing** — the lowest-cost option to extend a lot that's mostly healthy.

If you're not sure which you need, our [asphalt repair team](/services/asphalt-repair-services) evaluates the base before quoting, so you're not paying for a replacement when an overlay would last.

### 2. Asphalt thickness and base depth
A lot that only sees passenger cars needs less structure than one with daily delivery trucks, garbage trucks, or fire-lane access. Heavier traffic means a deeper compacted stone base and thicker asphalt lifts — more material and more labor. Spec'ing this correctly is where experience pays off: too thin and the lot fails early; too thick and you've overspent.

### 3. Drainage and grading
Standing water is the number-one killer of asphalt in Ohio. Lots that need re-grading, new catch basins, or drainage correction cost more up front but save you from premature failure. Per the [National Asphalt Pavement Association](https://www.asphaltpavement.org), proper drainage is one of the single biggest factors in pavement longevity.

### 4. Striping, ADA compliance, and site features
Fresh [line striping](/services/line-striping-services) is part of nearly every lot project, and commercial lots must meet [ADA accessible-parking requirements](https://www.ada.gov) for the number and layout of handicap spaces. Curbing, wheel stops, signage, and islands all add to the total.

### 5. Lot size and site access
Larger lots cost less per square foot (mobilization is spread over more area), while tight, obstructed, or phased sites — where you keep part of the lot open for business — cost more per square foot to work around.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does it cost to pave a small commercial lot in Central Ohio?**
A small lot of 5,000 square feet typically runs $15,000 to $35,000 for new asphalt, depending on base condition, thickness, and drainage needs. Resurfacing a sound 5,000-square-foot lot costs less. The only way to get an accurate number is an on-site evaluation, which we provide free.

**How long does it take to pave a parking lot?**
Most commercial lots are paved in 1 to 3 days depending on size and whether base work is needed. Full new construction with excavation takes longer. The surface is usually ready for traffic within 24 to 48 hours. We can phase larger lots so part stays open for business.

**When is the best time to pave a parking lot in Ohio?**
The ideal window is late April through October, when temperatures stay above 50°F so hot-mix asphalt compacts and bonds properly. We monitor conditions closely and won't pave when temperatures are borderline, because poor compaction shortens the life of the lot.

**Do you handle striping and ADA-compliant layouts?**
Yes. Every paving or resurfacing project includes professional [line striping](/services/line-striping-services), and we lay out ADA-compliant accessible spaces, fire lanes, and traffic flow to meet code. Faded or non-compliant striping is both a safety and a liability issue for Ohio businesses.

**How can I make my new parking lot last longer?**
Sealcoat every 2 to 3 years, seal cracks as soon as they appear, and keep drainage clear. Routine [sealcoating](/services/sealcoating-services) blocks water, UV, and chemical damage and can roughly double the life of the surface — the cheapest insurance there is on an asphalt investment.
